## Design Context

### Users
Poker Pace is for a beginner Hold'em player preparing for an offline no-limit Texas Hold'em tournament with friends in about one month. The user knows the basic rules but needs practical confidence: reading hands, choosing Fold/Call/Raise, understanding position, and remembering what to agree on before tournament day. The app is used in short phone sessions, often when the user wants to do a few more lessons or quizzes beyond the recommended next unit.

### Brand Personality
Focused, tactile, quietly competitive. Poker Pace should feel like a pocket training table: calm enough for study, sharp enough to feel like tournament preparation, and friendly enough for a beginner who is still building confidence.

### Aesthetic Direction
Mobile-first dark theme derived from evening practice and poker-table context, but not a casino app. The visual language should suggest felt, cards, chips, and study pace without neon, glamor, gambling tropes, or generic glowing dashboard aesthetics. It should feel like a compact training instrument rather than a marketing landing page.

### Design Principles
- Lead with the next useful action: the user should instantly know what to study or review.
- Make progress feel earned, not gamified: use measured completion, card states, and calm feedback.
- Keep poker visuals functional: cards, chips, and table texture should clarify context, not decorate randomly.
- Preserve beginner safety: explanations, empty states, and checklist copy should reduce uncertainty.
- Stay mobile ergonomic: large tap targets, fixed bottom navigation, readable dark UI, and no cramped controls.
